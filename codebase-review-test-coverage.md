## Area Reviewed

Test coverage review of Blockiverse-VR: inventoried all 56 test files (~494 [Test]/[UnityTest] methods, ~13.8K lines) across Assets/Blockiverse/Tests/EditMode (7 asmdefs incl. Survival, SurvivalHealth, Networking, MetaAvatars sub-assemblies) and Tests/PlayMode (incl. the 2,209-line real-Netcode MultiplayerSessionPlayModeTests), and traced them against the production systems they claim to cover (BlockMutationAuthority, MultiplayerChunkAuthoritySync, MultiplayerSurvivalSync, WorldSaveService, FarmingService, FluidFlowService, vitals, menus, session controllers). Overall assessment: the engine-free simulation core and the save format are excellently tested with meaningful, behavior-level assertions (not assert-doesn't-throw padding), and the Netcode PlayMode suite genuinely exercises host authority, late join, stale-request rejection, latency and packet-loss convergence with multi-peer topologies. The big weakness is the integration/glue layer above the models: the entire single-player session lifecycle (BlockiverseWorldSessionController — new world/save/load/continue/autosave), menu routing incl. the documented death→respawn→save ordering invariant (BlockiverseMenuController), the reconnect-identity inventory reclaim flow, the bucket/fluid economy commands, environment-snapshot lockstep for late joiners, and the live autosave loops have zero tests — and the one autosave check that IS tested (WorldSaveService.ShouldAutoSave) is dead code with no production callers. DeterministicHash, the keystone of save compatibility and cross-peer determinism, has self-consistency coverage but no golden-value regression tests.
